## Governance Process

### Guiding Principles

Our governance process aims to be:

* **Open** – Anyone in the community may propose improvements.
* **Transparent** – Discussions and decisions happen in public (in GitHub).
* **Collaborative** – We seek consensus through discussion before relying on approvals.
* **Practical** – The process should help us move forward, not create unnecessary bureaucracy.
* **Evolving** – As our community grows, we expect this process to grow with it.

### Proposal

Any community member may open a Pull Request proposing changes to community-owned assets, such as governance documents, the manifesto, the Code of Conduct, or other shared resources.

The Pull Request should clearly describe:

* the goal of the proposed change,
* the motivation behind it,
* and any relevant context.

### Community Review

Once opened, the proposal is announced on Discord to invite community participation.

GitHub is the canonical place for discussion, feedback, and approvals. Discord is used only for notification and to encourage participation.

Proposals should normally remain open for at least **2 full days** to allow members across different time zones and schedules to participate.

### Discussion and Refinement

Community members are encouraged to discuss the proposal, ask questions, and suggest improvements.

The goal of this stage is to build shared understanding and, where possible, reach consensus through constructive discussion.

If significant concerns are raised, the proposal should be revised to address them before moving forward.

### Acknowledgement

After the review period, a proposal may be merged when:

* it has received sufficient participation from community members,
* it has received broad approval from those who participated,
* and any substantive objections have been addressed or resolved.

Community members who do not participate during the review period are considered to have abstained.

The exact participation and approval thresholds may evolve as the community grows and gains experience with this process.

### Different Kinds of Decisions

Not every proposal requires the same level of scrutiny.

Routine editorial improvements or clarifications may follow a lighter review process, while changes affecting governance, the manifesto, membership, or other foundational documents should receive broader community review before being accepted.

The community will refine these categories over time as experience shows where additional structure is helpful.

### Continuous Improvement

This governance process is itself a community document.

We expect to revisit and improve it as we learn what works well. The objective is not to create rigid rules, but to support a healthy, transparent, and collaborative community that can continue to grow.
